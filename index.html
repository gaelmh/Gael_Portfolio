Predicting Healthcare Costs

This analysis aimed to improve financial transparency in healthcare by developing a machine learning model to estimate hospital total charges. The dataset was gathered from the New York Government Health Data website. It contained over two million patient discharges from 2017 and was described by 34 initial features which were refined into 139 features after preprocessing.

XGBoost demonstrated the best performance, achieving an R2 value of 0.85, with LightGBM and Neural Network also achieving high performance. A feature importance analysis revealed that "length_of_stay" was the most influential factor. Furthermore, the project further included the utilization of an interactive Shiny web application, allowing users to input personal and medical information to receive real-time cost predictions.

This research highlights the significant value of machine learning in healthcare cost prediction, offering crucial insights for patients, insurance companies, and hospitals to optimize financial planning and decision-making processes.

Boot Brand Preference Among Soccer Player

This analysis aimed to accurately predict the preferred boot brand among soccer players and identify the most influential factors affecting their choice. The study utilized the "Football Boots" dataset from Kaggle, containing 3,939 samples and 14 features after initial preprocessing and class reduction to the three main brands (Nike, Adidas, Puma).

Five machine learning models were trained, with the Decision Tree consistently performing best, achieving high accuracy and F1 score across different settings. Conversely, Ada Boosting was the weakest performer, with an accuracy of 54% and an F1 score of 41%. Feature importance analysis consistently identified "BootsTopPlayer" (indicating the most popular soccer player wearing those boots), "BootsPosition", and qq"BootsMaterial" as the most influential factors.

These findings are crucial for brands to enhance marketing campaigns and guide product development to meet evolving customer needs in the sportswear market.

Stock Exchange System

This project involved the design and implementation of a robust, object-oriented stock exchange simulation system in Java, developed iteratively across multiple assignments. The primary aim was to create a modular and scalable platform capable of managing financial instruments, processing trades, handling user interactions, and publishing real-time market data.

The system's architecture rigorously adhered to core Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) principles.
• Encapsulation was maintained through private data members and controlled access via public methods.
• Polymorphism was extensively utilized through interfaces like Comparable and Tradable, enabling flexible handling of diverse financial entities such as Price objects, Orders, and Quotes.
• Information hiding was paramount, with TradableDTO (Data Transfer Objects) used to safely convey data without exposing internal object references.

Furthermore, key design patterns were strategically applied to enhance functionality and maintainability:
• Factory Pattern: A PriceFactory centralized the creation of immutable Price objects, ensuring consistency and proper formatting.
• Flyweight Pattern: The PriceFactory was further optimized to implement the Flyweight pattern, ensuring that only a single Price object exists for any given value, significantly reducing memory footprint.
• Singleton Pattern: UserManager, ProductManager, CurrentMarketTracker, and CurrentMarketPublisher were implemented as Singletons to ensure a single, globally accessible instance for managing users, products, market data tracking, and publication, respectively.
• Facade Pattern: UserManager and ProductManager acted as Facades, simplifying complex interactions with underlying user and product book management subsystems.
• Observer Pattern: A CurrentMarketObserver interface, implemented by the User class, along with CurrentMarketPublisher and CurrentMarketTracker, enabled real-time, decoupled updates of market information to subscribed users.
This project is important as it demonstrates a comprehensive understanding of designing and building complex, real-world applications using advanced OOP concepts and design patterns, crucial for developing reliable and scalable financial systems.

Understanding Healthcare Costs

This study aimed to understand the relationship between demographic and lifestyle factors and their impact on medical costs. The data utilized for this analysis was the "Medical Costs Personal Dataset" from Kaggle, comprising 1,338 observations across 7 distinct features.

Linear regression revealed that all variables significantly influenced log medical costs, with smoking having the highest effect, followed by the number of children and region.
Logistic regression achieved 90.67% accuracy in predicting whether medical costs would exceed $9,500, highlighting age and region as statistically significant predictors.
Finally, ANOVA demonstrated a clear and statistically significant link between smoking and higher medical expenses, with smokers incurring, on average, $23,614 more in costs.

These findings offer valuable insights for healthcare practitioners, policymakers, and insurers, aiding in evidence-based decision-making for healthcare financing, resource allocation, and policy formulation, particularly regarding the financial burden of smoking.

NBA Free Throw Accuracy

This analysis studied the relationship between NBA players statistics like their position, free throw attempts, and their MVP status, with their free throw accuracy, utilizing a dataset of NBA player statistics gathered from Kaggle. Such dataset contained information from the 1997/98 to the 2021/22 seasons, containing 14573 observations and 32 features before data cleaning and preprocessing.

The relationship was mainly studied through the creation of data visualizations, which provided insights that include:
• Positional Influence: Point guards and shooting guards generally exhibit better free throw accuracy than centers and power forwards, suggesting a positional influence on free throw skills related to player roles.
• Attempts vs. Accuracy: Contrary to intuition, a higher number of free throw attempts showed a slight decrease in accuracy, indicating that other factors beyond sheer practice are involved.
• MVP Status: Players who have earned the MVP title are more accurate and more likely to score free throws than non-MVPs, despite the small number of MVP instances (approximately 25 MVPs in the dataset).

Overall, the visualizations demonstrated the complexity of free throw accuracy, influenced by multiple factors such as fatigue, pressure, and practice. The analysis also highlighted that players who score the most points are more likely to be selected as MVPs, reinforcing the importance of scoring ability in elite player recognition.
